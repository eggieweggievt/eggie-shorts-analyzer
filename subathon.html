🥚 Subathons, explained like you're new (because everyone is once)

A subathon is a stream where viewers add time to a countdown clock — every sub, donation, or cheer extends the stream. When the timer hits zero, the stream ends. A donothon is the same thing driven by donations, and a marathon is a long multi-day stream with a fixed schedule and no timer. This page handles all three.

Why do one? Subathons compress weeks of community energy into one event — they're hype, they fund big goals, and they're a milestone your community builds with you. The flow is simple:

  1. Set your rules — how much time each sub/dono adds, and (please) a hard cap.
  2. Set goals + incentives — what milestones unlock ("at 50 subs I do karaoke").
  3. Plan the schedule — blocks of games, chatting, art, collabs, breaks.
  4. Prep your body and your tech — food, sleep plan, mods, backups.
  5. Go live — add the timer widget to OBS and let chat take the wheel.

Work through the steps below in order. Everything saves as you go.

1 · The basics

💡 First subathon? Start the clock at 4–6 hours and cap the whole event at one weekend. A great first subathon is one you finish feeling proud, not destroyed — you can always go bigger next time.

2 · Timer rules

How much time does each event add? These power the quick-add buttons on the Go Live control panel. Typical starting points are pre-filled — tune them to your community's size.

🛟 The cap is not optional (okay, technically it is — but don't). Endless subathons sound fun until you're 30 hours in with chat still adding time. A cap protects your health AND gives the event a finale moment people show up for. Announce it up front: "the clock can never exceed 48 hours."

3 · Goals & incentives

🏆 Goal ladder

Milestones for the event total (subs, dollars — your call). Check them off live as they're hit. Keep early goals easy so momentum starts fast: a goal hit in the first hour is worth three hit on day two.

🎁 Incentives

What unlocks at each milestone — the reason people push the number. Mix free hype ("karaoke hour") with meaningful ones ("name on my model"). Only promise what you can actually deliver after the event.

4 · Day-by-day schedule

Block out each day in chunks: what you're doing, whether it's a collab, and whether it needs tech setup. You won't follow it perfectly — that's fine. The schedule exists so you never sit there at hour 9 wondering what to do next, and so collab partners know when to show up.

🧩 Block pattern that works: alternate high-energy (games, collabs) with low-energy (chatting, food breaks, "yap breaks"). Schedule real meal breaks ON the schedule — "dinner with chat" counts as content. End each day with something calm.

5 · Prep & safety

The unglamorous part that makes or breaks the event. Work through this in the week before.

❤️ Non-negotiable: if your event runs past ~16 hours, plan sleep. Sleeping on stream (timer paused or "sleep cam") is a beloved subathon tradition — your community will protect your rest. Pushing through multiple days without sleep isn't dedication, it's a health risk, and your viewers don't want it either.
