About the Analyzer 🐙

What's inside, how it picks the right tags, every game and niche the system already knows about — and how to ask me to add yours.

What it does

Eggie's Shorts Analyzer is a free tool for VTubers, streamers, and short-form creators. Upload a short, tell me what it's about (or let the system read the visuals and audio for you), and you get:

🎯 A score out of 10

Broken down by hook, duration, title, hashtags, caption, engagement triggers, SEO, niche fit, and retention — sorted by what moves the score most.

🏷️ Five smart hashtags

A 1 small / 2 medium / 2 large mix per the growth rubric, drawn from a topic pool that matches your specific content (not generic "#shorts" filler).

📋 A ready-to-copy post pack

Title + description for YouTube, caption for TikTok + Instagram, and a 280-char tweet for X. Hashtags editable before you copy.

How it works

The analyzer runs entirely in your browser. Your video doesn't get uploaded anywhere — the models load locally and process the frames on your computer.

The detection pipeline

👁️ Florence-2 vision model (×3 frames)

Samples 3 frames — opening (25%), middle (50%), closing (75%) — and runs both a rich scene caption and an OCR pass per frame. So it catches things like "anime character with shocked expression on split-screen" and reads any on-screen text like "POV: you walked in on…", caption overlays, chat boxes, or stream alerts. Duplicate overlays across frames get deduped.

🎙️ Whisper transcription (medium.en)

Upgraded from tiny.en to whisper-medium.en (~770MB) — substantially better at catching slang, character names, fast speech, and the mental-health / storytime phrasing that makes or breaks the right tags. Transcribes whatever's being said and hands it to the detector.

🎞️ Frame + audio analysis

Multi-frame motion / cut detection, audio loudness, BlazeFace face detection (on the middle frame), color vibe, aspect ratio. Used to auto-score your hook strength.

📝 Your typed text + pills (tier-1)

What you type in "What is your short about?" and the short-type pills (gaming clip, storytime, IRL storytime, reaction, comic dub, etc.) are tier-1 signal — they always win over what the video shows. Emojis you type get translated to keywords (💀 → funny / dead, 🥺 → wholesome / cute, 🍵 → drama / tea), and any #hashtags you drop in get extracted as explicit topic signals too.

First analysis downloads the models (~380MB total). After that they're cached in your browser, so subsequent shorts run faster and stay fully offline.

The 1S / 2M / 2L hashtag formula

Every short gets 5 hashtags, mixed by reach size. Same formula used in the growth rubric — gives your video a fighting chance at every stage of the algorithm.

S 1 small (under 500k posts) — low competition, easier to rank in M 2 medium (500k to 1M) — balanced reach L 2 large (1M+) — high exposure if you break out

If your topic doesn't have tags in every size, the picker fills with the best mix it can and tells you it didn't hit the formula exactly.

Variety, not topic-clustering

The 5 picks come from 5 different slots so the mix is rich: primary topic (e.g. #mentalhealth) → VTuber identity if you're a VTuber → stream/clip identity if relevant → content type (#storytime, #funny, #pov…) → sub-topic from your topic's pool (e.g. #therapy when "therapy" appears in your text). Then the size quota tunes which exact tag is picked from each slot.

The fine print

Is this affiliated with YouTube / TikTok / Instagram / VidIQ?

No. The growth rubric is based on publicly-known short-form best practices + outlier research I've done on hashtags that actually perform. Tag pools are refreshed regularly using VidIQ data.

Is it actually free?

Yes. There's a Ko-fi link on the main page if you want to support it, but you don't have to — I built it for the VTuber + streamer community to use.
