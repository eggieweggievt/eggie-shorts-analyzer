About the Analyzer 🐙

What's inside, how it picks the right tags, every game and niche the system already knows about — and how to ask me to add yours.

What it does

Eggie's Shorts Analyzer is a free, in-browser tool for VTubers, streamers, and short-form creators. Upload a short, tell me what it's about (or let the system read the visuals and audio for you), and you get:

🎯 A score out of 10

Broken down by hook, duration, title, hashtags, caption, engagement triggers, SEO, niche fit, and retention — sorted by what moves the score most.

🏷️ Five smart hashtags

A 1 small / 2 medium / 2 large mix per the growth rubric, drawn from a topic pool that matches your specific content (not generic "#shorts" filler).

📋 A ready-to-copy post pack

Title + description for YouTube, caption for TikTok + Instagram, and a 280-char tweet for X. Hashtags editable before you copy.

How it works

The analyzer runs entirely in your browser. Your video doesn't get uploaded anywhere — the models load locally and process the frames on your computer.

The detection pipeline

👁️ Florence-2 vision model (×3 frames)

Samples 3 frames — opening (25%), middle (50%), closing (75%) — and runs both a rich scene caption and an OCR pass per frame. So it catches things like "anime character with shocked expression on split-screen" and reads any on-screen text like "POV: you walked in on…", caption overlays, chat boxes, or stream alerts. Duplicate overlays across frames get deduped.

🎙️ Whisper transcription (base.en)

Upgraded from tiny.en to whisper-base.en (~140MB) — substantially better at catching slang, character names, fast speech, and the mental-health / storytime phrasing that makes or breaks the right tags. Transcribes whatever's being said and hands it to the detector.

🎞️ Frame + audio analysis

Multi-frame motion / cut detection, audio loudness, BlazeFace face detection (on the middle frame), color vibe, aspect ratio. Used to auto-score your hook strength.

📝 Your typed text + pills (tier-1)

What you type in "What is your short about?" and the short-type pills (gaming clip, storytime, IRL storytime, reaction, comic dub, etc.) are tier-1 signal — they always win over what the video shows. Emojis you type get translated to keywords (💀 → funny / dead, 🥺 → wholesome / cute, 🍵 → drama / tea), and any #hashtags you drop in get extracted as explicit topic signals too.

First analysis downloads the models (~380MB total). After that they're cached in your browser, so subsequent shorts run faster and stay fully offline.

The 1S / 2M / 2L hashtag formula

Every short gets 5 hashtags, mixed by reach size. Same formula used in the growth rubric — gives your video a fighting chance at every stage of the algorithm.

S 1 small (under 500k posts) — low competition, easier to rank in M 2 medium (500k to 1M) — balanced reach L 2 large (1M+) — high exposure if you break out

If your topic doesn't have tags in every size, the picker fills with the best mix it can and tells you it didn't hit the formula exactly.

Variety, not topic-clustering

The 5 picks come from 5 different slots so the mix is rich: primary topic (e.g. #mentalhealth) → VTuber identity if you're a VTuber → stream/clip identity if relevant → content type (#storytime, #funny, #pov…) → sub-topic from your topic's pool (e.g. #therapy when "therapy" appears in your text). Then the size quota tunes which exact tag is picked from each slot.

📊 How the algorithm actually works

Every social-media platform has one goal: keep people scrolling. The algorithm is a matchmaker between content and viewers. When you post a video, the platform builds a "digital fingerprint" of it using:

  • Computer vision — what's happening visually
  • Audio fingerprinting — what's being said
  • Metadata — captions, hashtags, creator info

Then it picks ~200 people to test your video on first. Most of those 200 are non-followers — the algorithm wants to see if strangers like it. If they engage, the video gets pushed to 2,000 → 20,000 → 200,000 in waves. If they don't, the push slows or stops immediately.

What this means for you: the first few seconds of performance are everything. Posting to the wrong audience = bad data = the algorithm stops pushing. This is why niching down is critical — it keeps your test sample consistent. One viral hit to the WRONG audience can tank your next several videos. Followers matter less than you think — strangers engaging is the real signal.
🎯 The 3 metrics that actually matter
  • Average watch time + completion rate — how long viewers stay, and what % finish
  • Engagement rate — likes + comments + shares ÷ views
  • Watchtime session share — how much of a user's TOTAL session was YOUR content. You can't see this anywhere in your analytics dashboard, but the algorithm weighs it heavily.
✅ The 4-criteria content rule

Your video needs to hit all four of these to get pushed:

  • Relevant — solves a real problem your target viewer has
  • Non-obvious — says something they haven't heard before
  • Absorbable — communicated clearly enough to actually understand
  • Actionable — they can implement it and get a result
📺 YouTube Shorts specifics
  • Shorts are fed through a queue — no impressions, just views vs. swipes
  • Aim for 45–55 seconds with 85–90% retention for best results
  • Don't go under 25 seconds unless your short is extremely rewatchable
  • The "viewed vs. swiped" stat is your most important early metric — aim for 75%+
  • Edit your shorts. Always. Auto-converting landscape clips does not work — vertical-first or it dies
  • Watch shorts in your niche for 1–2 hours a week — study what's working
💬 Driving more comments (algorithm boost)
  • Take a hard stance — people comment when they strongly agree OR disagree
  • Pick the contrarian side when you can
  • Ratchet up your framing — be bold, not wishy-washy
  • Talk about cult-loved brands, games, or communities people already have opinions on
  • Make people FEEL something — emotion drives comments more than logic

🏷️ The hashtag formula (built into the analyzer)

Always use a mix of small / medium / large tags so your content can perform at ANY stage of the algorithm:

  • Small tags (under 500k posts) — low competition, easier to rank in. Your easy-win slot.
  • Medium tags (500k – 1M) — balanced reach. Most of your discoverability lives here.
  • Large tags (1M+) — high exposure if you break out. Don't lean on these alone or you'll get buried.

Standard mix: 1 small / 2 medium / 2 large. If you already have audience traction, lean more on medium + large.

What the analyzer does: picks 5 tags per platform from a curated pool, enforces the 1S/2M/2L mix, and shows you the size badge on every pick so you can verify the formula was hit.
🌱 Growing on Twitch (the real strategy)

Twitch's discovery system is broken for small streamers. Categories are sorted highest viewers first — you get buried. Streaming alone and hoping people find you will not work. You need to build your audience BEFORE relying on Twitch to push you.

The actual strategy:

  • Build your presence on a platform with a working algorithm first (TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels)
  • Funnel viewers from short-form into your stream
  • Even 1% click-through compounds over time
  • More viewers → higher in category → Twitch starts pushing you organically

Niching down is non-negotiable:

  • "Variety streamer" puts you in a box with 300,000 other people
  • You need to be describable in one sentence
  • Example: "I play Souls games, love Warhammer, and give VTuber tips"
  • Pick games with viewers but LOW streamer competition — use TwitchTracker to find peak ratio time slots

Mindset shift:

  • Consistency ≠ streaming every day — it means reliable patterns
  • Don't chase trends blindly — find where trends OVERLAP with your content
  • Passion alone grabs attention. Passion + strategy keeps it.
  • You don't need to go viral. You need to compound.
📝 Title strategy (this matters more than you think)

New viewers only see your title before clicking. A good title creates curiosity, shock, or confusion — anything that makes them stop scrolling.

Format that works on Twitch:

[Aesthetic emoji/decoration] + [Hook/Meme] + [Game] + [VTuber] + [Commands]

  • "Chill vibes" tells nobody anything — give them a reason to click
  • Challenges work great: "If I die, I restart" beats "cozy stream" every time
  • "24 hour stream" creates instant curiosity
  • Questions work extremely well — "HE DID WHAT WITH THAT SPATULA?!"
  • Goal: create confusion, shock, or curiosity → drive clicks
🗣️ Getting chatters actually engaged

If a new viewer types in chat they're 50% more likely to return. Welcome EVERY new chatter — sounds obvious but most streamers don't do it.

  • Use open-ended questions, not yes/no — "What's your favorite game from childhood that still holds up?" not "do you like this game?"
  • Put a question or hot take IN your stream title to prime engagement before they click
  • Stream Elements timers — remind viewers of commands and follows on a loop
  • Loyalty points + commands like !gamble or !heist keep chat participating
  • Fun commands like !iq or !based give chatters something to spam at each other
  • Words on Stream, Gartic, or chat-controlled game mods spike engagement fast — these also trick the algorithm into thinking your chat is more active than it is
Community culture takes time. You cannot force or fake it. Encourage in-jokes when they appear naturally. A slow chat does NOT mean you're boring — lurkers are the backbone of Twitch. Focus on making great content first; chat activity follows.

Stream structure tips:

  • Have a routine 20 minutes before going live — helps you show up consistently
  • Introduce yourself every time someone new follows or raids — "Hi I'm [name], I play [X], love [Y], welcome to the cult"
  • Always be plugging what you're working toward — fundraisers, goals, milestones
🔄 Conversion + retention (don't treat short-form as just a funnel)

Core mindset shift: if you only post to drive people elsewhere, you'll struggle on ALL platforms. Learn each platform for what it IS — growth on those will naturally convert. People who bond with you on short-form will seek out your stream on their own. You don't need constant CTAs — if they like you, they'll find you.

Why short-form converts at all:

  • Short-form gives viewers a concentrated taste of your personality
  • They see the edited, best-of version of you first
  • If they like THAT, they'll want the unfiltered live version
  • Make sure your short-form and stream personality are consistent — high-energy shorts + dead-silent stream = they leave

What actually converts:

  • Stream schedule clearly visible in your bio everywhere
  • Content backlog — new viewers want old videos to binge
  • Discord ready BEFORE you go viral — people need somewhere to land
  • Consistent upload schedule signals you're worth following long-term
The retention problem: when you blow up, new viewers immediately ask — Who is this? Discord? Old videos? Schedule? If the answer to any of those is no, they leave. Don't panic and change your content after a growth spike — you'll lose what made them click in the first place. Growth without retention is just a spike on a graph.

Funneling YouTube → Twitch:

  • Don't start your YouTube stream until you begin gaming — YouTube hates category switches mid-stream
  • Do your Just Chatting / reaction segment first, THEN start the YouTube VOD
  • Keeps your YouTube content clean and focused for the algorithm
  • Clip your stream highlights and post them as shorts — double duty

Content that travels: your model is a costume — the CONTENT is what gets viewership. Always ask: "Would someone who has never heard of VTubing still find this funny, useful, or interesting?" If no, go back to the drawing board. Reaction videos, gaming fails, hot takes, and unhinged opinions travel furthest. Inside jokes and VTuber-only memes keep your growth flatlined.

💰 Monetization + sponsors (you're a virtual busker)

Mindset first: you are not begging your audience — you are a virtual busker. Buskers perform, and if you like them, you throw coins in the case. Entertainment has value. You are allowed to ask for support. You earned it by showing up. Shift from "I hope someone donates" to "here's what we're working towards."

Diversify your income — don't rely on one source:

  • Twitch subs + bits
  • YouTube ad revenue
  • Sponsorships + affiliate codes
  • Merchandise
  • Tip jar / StreamElements
  • Throne wishlist
  • Paid collaborations

Getting sponsors — where to start:

  • Audit your socials first — sponsors will look before they respond
  • Remove or avoid: sad posting, drama, heavy personal venting on your main
  • Keep branding consistent across ALL platforms — everything should look like it came from the same character
  • Make sure your email and a link to your media kit are easy to find
  • A Carrd or Linktree is the minimum — a full media kit is better

Your media kit should include:

  • Your creator name + region
  • Follower counts across all platforms
  • Average viewership + total views
  • Engagement rate
  • Audience age range
  • Content genres + popular videos
  • Past collaborations and partnerships
  • Current and past sponsors

Writing your sponsor letter:

  • Treat it like a cover letter — sell yourself in the best light
  • Include: who you are, what platforms you're on, your content type, your best stats
  • Add a personal story related to the brand — makes you memorable
  • Keep a generic template and customize per sponsor
  • Worst they can say is no — doors reopen

Where to find sponsors:

  • Twitter — keep an eye on new game releases and partner programs
  • Publisher partnerships — Bandai Namco, Capcom, etc. all have creator programs
  • Lurkit — sign up for game keys + paid sponsorship opportunities
  • Affiliate portals — most brands have them at the bottom of their website
  • This community! (Eggie's Discord)
  • Don't wait to be approached — pitch first, pitch often
Vetting your sponsors matters. Only partner with brands you have genuine experience with or can endorse. If something goes wrong with a sponsor it can fall back on YOU. If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is. You're trusting them with your brand just as much as they're trusting you.

Small channel? You still qualify. CCV is not the only metric that matters. A small but engaged audience is valuable to the right brand. You can guest at conventions, land collabs, and get sponsors at any size. The difference between those who get opportunities and those who don't? They applied. That's it. Apply.

The fine print

Where does my video go?

Nowhere. Everything runs in your browser. The vision and audio models download once (about ~310MB total, cached after that), then all processing happens locally on your computer. The site never sees your video.

Is this affiliated with YouTube / TikTok / Instagram / VidIQ?

No. The growth rubric is based on publicly-known short-form best practices + outlier research I've done on hashtags that actually perform. Tag pools are refreshed regularly using VidIQ data.

Is it actually free?

Yes. There's a Ko-fi link on the main page if you want to support it, but you don't have to — I built it for the VTuber + streamer community to use.
